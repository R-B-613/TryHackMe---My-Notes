Summary:

The components of the OPSEC process:
Identifying critical information - Critical Information:
There is a need to identify critical information in order to know how to protect it, and at the same time know how to reach it as an adversary.
There is basic critical information, and there is critical information that depends on the assets or tools being used.

Threat analysis - threats:
We need to know who the adversary is, what their goals are, what TTPs they use, and what information they have obtained, if any.

Vulnerability analysis - vulnerabilities:
We need to check what information, if exposed, could cause the adversary to get information that will help them attack effectively and reach sensitive information.

Performing risk assessment - risks:
Learning the possibility of an event occurring along with the expected cost of that event. This way we can assess the adversary's ability to exploit the vulnerabilities.
If the red team wants to do action X, and it is known that the adversary has tools at a level that can detect this action, it is called a high risk because there is a high probability of detecting the action.

Applying countermeasures - countermeasures:
Designed to prevent an adversary from identifying critical information, provide an alternative interpretation to critical information or indicators (deception), or deny the adversary's collection system.

Implementing the 5 stages of OPSEC - I successfully classified a list of cases into the correct stages:
1. Identify critical information
2. Analyze threats
3. Analyze vulnerabilities
4. Assess risk
5. Apply appropriate countermeasures

Detailed stages from the room:
Identify critical information: “Critical information includes, but is not limited to, red team’s intentions, capabilities, activities and limitations.”
Analyse threats: Threat analysis refers to identifying potential adversaries and their intentions and capabilities.
Analyse vulnerabilities: An OPSEC vulnerability exists when an adversary can obtain critical information, analyse the findings, and act in a way that would affect your plans.
Assess risks: “Risk assessment requires learning the possibility of an event taking place along with the expected cost of that event.”
Apply appropriate countermeasures: Countermeasures are designed to prevent an adversary from detecting critical information, provide an alternative interpretation of critical information or indicators (deception), or deny the adversary’s collection system.
